When evaluating large language models (LLMs), the underlying features dictate how they can be applied to real-world use cases. Here is how Temuclaude and Gemini 3.5 stack up against each other.

**Key Takeaway:** Gemini 3.5 dominates in raw context length and multimodal processing, making it ideal for analyzing massive documents or video files. Temuclaude, however, excels in maintaining a consistent, human-like conversational tone over long interactions.

## Pricing Breakdown

Cost is a critical factor for developers and enterprises scaling AI applications. Both models offer tiered pricing structures, but they cater to slightly different budgets.

### Temuclaude Pricing
*   **Free Tier:** Limited to 50 queries per day with standard response speeds.
*   **Pro Tier:** $15/month for general users. Includes priority access and higher token limits.
*   **API Pricing:** $0.004 per 1,000 input tokens / $0.012 per 1,000 output tokens. 
*   **Enterprise:** Custom pricing with dedicated support and on-premise deployment options.

### Google Gemini 3.5 Pricing
*   **Free Tier:** Integrated into Google Workspace (personal accounts) with strict daily usage caps.
*   **Advanced Tier:** $19.99/month (bundled with Google One AI Premium and 2TB storage).
*   **API Pricing:** $0.007 per 1,000 input tokens / $0.021 per 1,000 output tokens.
*   **Enterprise:** Billed via Google Cloud Vertex AI, with pricing based on compute resources used.

**Key Takeaway:** Temuclaude is the clear winner for budget-conscious developers and startups. Gemini 3.5 is slightly more expensive but offers immense value if your team already relies heavily on the Google Cloud infrastructure.
